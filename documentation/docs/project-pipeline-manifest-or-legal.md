# Project Pipeline Manifest | Legal

## PROJECT LEGAL MANIFEST & COMPLIANCE BLUEPRINT

DOCUMENT PURPOSE: This document establishes the legal manifest, technical boundary protocols, and risk mitigation strategies for the procedural generation, modeling, and publication of four distinct virtual environment modules: Washington D.C., the Gold Coast City LGA, the Australian Parliament House (APH) in Canberra, and the Brisbane 2032 Sports Precinct. This document serves as an internal compliance standard for integration across Confluence, GitBooks, Google Docs, and project configuration repositories.

### 1. EXECUTIVE PROJECT SCOPE & INTENT

The project involves generating wide-scale, 1:1 geographically accurate, three-dimensional (3D) digital environments designed as a continuous, unrestricted "playable background" or interactive simulation space. The world generation code runs uniformly and non-discriminately across entire local government areas (LGAs) and city grids, allowing unhindered physical freedom of movement for players across both public and private spaces without arbitrary artificial barriers.

#### Module 1: Washington D.C. Simulation

* Intent: Replication of the municipal street grid, public infrastructure, and structural massing of the U.S. capital city.
* Boundary Execution: Strict demarcation between public domain facades and protected, classified, or security-sensitive federal interior blueprints.

#### Module 2: Gold Coast City LGA

* Intent: Procedural reconstruction of the continuous Gold Coast municipal layout, coastal strip, and internal urban networks based entirely on open-source geographic profiles.
* Boundary Execution: Unrestricted physical exploration using automated, non-discriminatory data layouts. High-detail mapping is isolated to public corridors, while private commercial enclaves retain completely unobscured but entirely de-branded, generic structural representations.

#### Module 3: Australian Parliament House (APH) - Canberra

* Intent: Precise 1:1 modeling of the iconic capital hill layout, surrounding public loops, access roads, and architectural exterior masses.
* Boundary Execution: The external architecture is accurately reproduced as visible from public zones. Internal chambers, lobbies, and accessible public halls are situated correctly according to public records but built using generic, non-proprietary assets to prevent security or custom blueprint infringement.

#### Module 4: Brisbane 2032 Sports & Urban Precinct

* Intent: Visualization of future infrastructure developments, mass transit paths, and structural upgrades mapped for the city of Brisbane.
* Boundary Execution: Complete insulation from highly protected international sporting hallmarks, focusing strictly on civil architecture and public geography.

### 2. PROCEDURAL, PERFORMANCE, & TECHNICAL PIPELINE

To achieve performance targets and ensure absolute legal insulation, the development workflow implements a non-discriminatory, data-driven procedural generation pipeline.

#### Step 1: Macro-Level Automated Generation (Global Blockout)

The environment ingestion script loads commercially licensed Digital Elevation Models (DEM) and Geographic Information System (GIS) shapefiles globally across the entire local target area without property-by-property discrimination. The engine runs an automated extrusion algorithm that processes building footprints as topographical facts, translating height data into basic low-to-medium-poly structural blocks. Vegetation and tree clusters are spawned procedurally across the global map by referencing public environmental data layers that index existing natural grids.

#### Step 2: Texture Downsampling & Optimization

Global aerial and satellite photography is downsampled to medium resolution for the vast majority of the map layout to maximize system rendering efficiency and maintain performance during air vehicle flights or flyovers. High-resolution original imagery is retained strictly at ground level along key public play zones and primary driving avenues to ensure immediate visual clarity within the active user zones.

#### Step 3: Localized Re-Instancing & De-Branding Shaders

Standard, high-fidelity modular assets (curbs, streetlights, public benches) are manually or semi-procedurally re-instanced along public roadways to enhance aesthetic depth. Shaders automatically scrub out or blur any corporate brand identifiers, trademarked logos, or custom commercial signage captured by aerial imagery.

Real-world proprietary structures within private boundaries (such as specific engineered ride properties or specialized corporate park layouts) are left out of the raw data import. They are replaced by generic, unbranded 3D assets of the same _functional type_ (e.g., a standard looping steel coaster layout or a generic twin-arm flat ride model), utilizing alternative color schemes and generic station structures to fully dissolve similarity to the source entities without obscuring the visibility of the terrain or geometry.

### 3. CORE LEGAL FOUNDATIONS & BOUNDARY CONDITIONS

#### 3.1 Fact vs. Expression and Automated Compilation

The foundational protection for the automated pipeline rests on the fact that raw geographical coordinates, building dimensions, and environmental topography constitute non-copyrightable data. In Australian law, the landmark decision in [IceTV Pty Ltd v Nine Network Australia Pty Ltd (2009)](https://www.google.com/search?q=%23ref-icetv) solidified the precedent that purely factual info, listings, and automated data compilations lack the creative human authorship required to be protected by copyright. Copyright protection requires a deliberate, original human creative expression. Because the generation software treats the landscape uniformly without targeted human replication of a property's internal blueprint, the resulting layout represents an array of geographical facts, completely insulated from claims of creative plagiarism.

#### 3.2 Freedom of Panorama

When rendering the 3D structures visible from the air or public pathways, the project operates under strict Freedom of Panorama statutes across both jurisdictions:

* Australian Territory: Guided by [Section 66 of the Copyright Act 1968 (Cth)](https://www.google.com/search?q=%23ref-cth-panorama), the copyright of a building or a model of a building is not infringed by its inclusion in a visual broadcast, cinematograph film, or digital simulation environment, provided it is permanently situated in a public place.
* United States Territory: Supported by the [Architectural Works Copyright Protection Act (1990) (17 U.S. Code § 120)](https://www.google.com/search?q=%23ref-us-panorama), which allows the making, distribution, or public display of pictures, paintings, or visual representations of an architectural work if the building is located in or ordinarily observable from a public space.

#### 3.3 Private Property Boundaries & Intellectual Property Masking

While a player may have no artificial boundary walls preventing entry into a virtual space, the legal integrity of that space is maintained by stripping out proprietary human expressions. The world remains fully visible and interactive, but fine-detail path configurations are smoothed into generic layouts, and internal proprietary components are replaced with standard, unbranded marketplace assets to prevent trademark infringement or passing off under the [Competition and Consumer Act 2010 (Cth)](https://www.google.com/search?q=%23ref-acl).

### 4. PROJECT-SPECIFIC ADAPTATIONS & COMPLIANCE

#### 4.1 Washington D.C.

The external layout uses raw public GIS mapping parameters. To comply with post-9/11 federal restrictions regarding the structural blueprints of sensitive security facilities, all internal layouts for spaces like the White House, the Capitol, or transit hubs are entirely fictionalized, genericized, or left inaccessible to prevent national security or espionage concerns under federal enforcement guidelines.

#### 4.2 Gold Coast City LGA (Proprietary Case Reference)

The global generation script processes commercial boundaries (such as local theme parks, resorts, and private commercial estates) identically to standard residential zones. To comply with Australian trademark laws under the [Trade Marks Act 1995 (Cth)](https://www.google.com/search?q=%23ref-trademark), all proprietary attractions, names, and custom-designed infrastructure are replaced with unbranded, functional equivalents. The continuous map layout remains unobstructed, serving as a large, playable geographic background that can be upgraded with specific high-detail licensed assets if a formal sponsorship or permission agreement is executed in the future.

#### 4.3 Australian Parliament House (APH)

The external geometry of APH, including its unique grass-covered ramps and central flagpole structure, is fully protected under public architecture provisions of [Section 66 of the Copyright Act 1968 (Cth)](https://www.google.com/search?q=%23ref-cth-panorama). However, custom installations—specifically the 196-square-meter Forecourt Mosaic designed by indigenous artist Jagamara Nelson—are subject to distinct artistic copyright protections and are replaced in-engine with a generic stone aggregate texture. Internal ministerial wings, underground security parking, and operational corridors are completely genericized to ensure full compliance with the [Parliamentary Precincts Act 1988 (Cth)](https://www.google.com/search?q=%23ref-precincts) and federal security protocols managed by the Department of Parliamentary Services.

#### 4.4 Brisbane 2032 Sports Precinct

The project documents public municipal infrastructure modifications and urban planning layouts for the city of Brisbane. Under the [Olympic Insignia Protection Act 1987 (Cth)](https://www.google.com/search?q=%23ref-olympic), the International Olympic Committee (IOC) and Australian Olympic Committee (AOC) maintain a strict monopoly over statutory expressions. To bypass this, the project enforces an absolute ban on protected terms (e.g., _Olympic, Olympics, Brisbane 2032_) and the Olympic Rings icon within all game assets, marketing, and filenames. The module must be framed and marketed exclusively as an unbranded urban development and civil engineering simulation.
